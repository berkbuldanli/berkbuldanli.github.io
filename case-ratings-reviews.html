Marketplace · Monetization

A new revenue stream from ratings & reviews

How I helped turn Delivery Hero's user-generated ratings and reviews from a purely informational feature into a monetizable product — and a 6% lift in UGC-related mCVR across 20+ markets.

Role
Product Manager, cross-regional
Scope
APAC · Europe · MENA (20+ markets)
Headline result
+6% UGC-related mCVR

The context

Ratings and reviews are one of the quietest but most important surfaces in a food-delivery marketplace. They're how millions of customers across Delivery Hero's markets decide what to order — the difference between tapping "order" and bouncing to a competitor. But user-generated content like this is usually treated as a cost centre: something you moderate and display, not something that earns its keep.

The problem

I owned the ratings-and-reviews domain across APAC, Europe, and MENA. Two things were true at once: the content was driving real conversion, and it wasn't contributing to revenue. The question I kept coming back to was whether we could unlock a new revenue stream from this content without eroding the trust that made it valuable in the first place — because the moment reviews feel bought, they stop working.

Discovery & approach

I anchored the work in the job the content actually does for a customer: reducing the risk of a bad order. That framing kept every idea honest — anything that made reviews less trustworthy was off the table, no matter how well it monetized.

  • Monetization design: pioneered monetization strategies for the UGC surface, structured so the customer-facing value stayed intact.
  • AI-assisted relevance: layered in AI — including ChatGPT-based functionality — to personalize what each customer sees and to improve the ranking algorithms behind reviews.
  • One roadmap, many markets: shipped a single direction that still flexed to local habits, languages, and expectations across 20+ countries.
  • Data-driven prioritization: used customer feedback and performance data to sequence which bets shipped first.

The decision & tradeoffs

The central tradeoff was monetization versus trust. Rather than chase the most aggressive revenue model, I prioritized approaches that improved the customer experience first — sharper personalization, better-ranked content — and let the revenue follow from a surface people still believed in. Slower on paper, but durable.

The outcome

The work opened a brand-new revenue stream from content that had never contributed to the top line before, and drove a 6% increase in UGC-related mCVR over two years — while keeping the ratings and reviews people rely on trustworthy.

What I'd carry forward

The lesson that stuck: monetization and user trust aren't opposites if you start from the customer's job. Anchoring the roadmap in "help people avoid a bad order" made the revenue model an outcome of a better product, not a tax on it — and that's the version that lasts.
