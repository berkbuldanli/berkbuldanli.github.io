About

Systems thinker, turned product manager.

I'm a product owner at Chainels in Rotterdam, where I lead strategy and delivery across the Platform and Integrations teams. The path here ran through some of the busiest marketplaces and e-commerce platforms in Türkiye — Delivery Hero, Getir, and Penti — and it started in industrial engineering, which is probably why I still think in systems and sequences before I think in features.

Along the way I've migrated two decades of company data onto a global platform, built a ratings-and-reviews product that opened a new revenue stream across 20+ markets, and shaped search and recommendation engines for millions of shoppers. I later added a master's in Big Data Analytics to put more rigor behind the data side of the work.

I care about discovery grounded in the real job a customer is trying to get done, decisions backed by data, and — above all — shipping things that actually make it to production.

How I work

A few principles I keep coming back to.

The habits that tend to survive contact with real roadmaps and real deadlines.

01

Start from the job to be done

Features are a means, not the point. I anchor discovery in the task a customer is actually trying to get done, so we build the thing behind the request — not just the request.

02

Decide with data, ship with judgment

Dashboards and experiments sharpen the odds, but someone still has to make the call. I use data to narrow the options, then commit and learn in production.

03

One roadmap, many markets

Working across 20+ countries teaches you to hold a shared product vision and local reality at the same time — a single direction that still respects how each market actually behaves.

04

Get it to production

A plan that never ships helps no one. I stay close to engineering, design, and QA through release, because the value only shows up once it's live in front of users.
